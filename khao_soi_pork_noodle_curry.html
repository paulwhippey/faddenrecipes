Khao Soi Pork Noodle Curry with Pickled Shallots

For the pickled shallots (optional)

  • 1 red chilli, finely chopped
  • 5 shallots, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 150ml white wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp sea salt

    • Main meal ingredients

  • low-calorie cooking spray
  • 1 level tbsp Thai red curry paste
  • 3 tsp mild curry powder
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 100ml reduced-fat coconut milk
  • 700ml chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp Thai fish sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 600g lean pork fillet (tenderloin), visible fat removed, thinly sliced
  • 300g dried egg noodles
  • roughly chopped fresh coriander, to serve
  • 4 spring onions thinly sliced, to serve

    • Method

    1. (Optional) First pickle the shallots. Put the chilli and shallots in a small non-reactive bowl (glass, ceramic or plastic) and pour over the vinegar. Add the salt and leave to pickle slowly at room temperature (it will take 30-40 minutes). Drain and set aside.

    2. Meanwhile, spray a large saucepan with low-calorie cooking spray and place over a medium-low heat. Add the curry paste and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, then add the curry powder and chilli flakes. Cook for 1 minute and then add the coconut milk, stock, fish sauce and lime juice and bring to a simmer. Add the pork and cook for 15 to 20 minutes or until cooked through and tender.

    3. While the pork is cooking, cook the noodles according to the pack instructions, rinse and drain well.

    4. Spoon the curry and noodles into shallow bowls, scatter over the coriander and spring onions (optionally: serve hot with the pickled shallots) and your favourite vegetables.
