Beef steak rice bowl with spicy Korean-style sauce

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp tamari (Japanese soy sauce)
  • 4 tbsp tomato puree
  • 1/2 tsp shichimi togarashi (7 spice)
  • 2 flat iron steaks
  • 2 spring onions, sliced
  • 60g radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 baby cucumber, spiralized
  • 150g sweetcorn (drained)
  • 250g steamed brown rice (microwave pack)
  • 2 tsp black sesame seeds
  • Sea salt

    • Method

    1. Make the Korean-style sauce. In a bowl, combine the vinegar, honey, tamari, tomato puree and shichimi togarashi (to taste, as it's spicy!)

    2. Heat a frying pan with 1/2 tbsp of oil on a medium heat. Season the steaks on both sides with sea salt, then place in the pan. Cook until golden brown (as a guide: 2 minutes on each side for medium-rare or 4 minutes on each side for well-done). Pour in the sauce and cook together for a further 2 minutes. Turn off the heat and leave in the pan to keep warm.

    3. Slice the spring onions. Thinly slice the radishes. Cut both ends off the cucumber then, sing a peeler, slice the cucumber into long, thin strips (use a julienne peeler or spiralizer, if you have one).

    4. Drain the sweetcorn. Heat a second large frying pan with 1/2 tbsp oil on a medium heat. Add the rice, spring onions, sweetcorn and half the sesame seeds. Cook for 3 minutes, stirring occasionally.

    5. Thinly slice the steaks and serve with the sweetcorn rice, cucumber and radishes. Drizzle the sauce over the steaks; sprinkle with the remaining sesame seeds.
