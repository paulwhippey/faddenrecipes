Sliced duck, Asian-style peach and noodle salad

Ingredients

  • 2 duck breasts
  • 80g buckwheat noodles
  • 80g sugar snap peas, cut in half lengthways
  • 1 baby cucumber, cut into half moons
  • 1 peach, cut into wedges
  • 1 lime, halved and juiced
  • 4cm fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red chilli, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp tamari soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Handful fresh mint, torn

    • Method

    1. Preheat the oven to fan 180 degrees C. Boil a kettle. Heat a dry frying pan on a medium heat, then place the duck (skin-side down) into the pan. Cook for 10 minutes, until the fat melts and the skin turns golden and crisp, then transfer to a small overnproof dish (skin-side up) and roast for 10 minutes, or until centre is 52 degrees C using a meat thermometer.

    2. Meanwhile, fill a large saucepan three-quarters full with boiling water. Bring to the boil, then add the noodles, cooking for 5 minutes. Drain and rinse under cold water to separate.

    3. Cut the sugar snaps in half lengthways. Thinly slice the cucumber into half-moons. Cut the peach in half, remove the stone, then slice into wedges. Halve the lime.

    4. Heat a second frying pan with 1/2 tbsp of oil on a medium-high heat. Add the peach and sugar snaps, cooking for 3 minutes until softening. Turn off the heat.

    5. Make the tamari dressing. Peel the ginger and grate. Finly slice the chilli (remove the seeds for less heat). Add both to a large bowl with the sesame oil, tamari soy sauce, honey and juice from the lime.

    6. Add the peach, sugar snaps, noodles and cucumber to the bowl with the dressing. Tear the mint leaves and gently mix to combine. Thinly slice the duck and serve with the noodles.
