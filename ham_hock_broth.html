Lentil, ham hock and cavolo nero broth

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 leek, trimmed and finely sliced
  • 1 celery stalk, finely diced
  • 1 carrot, finely diced
  • 1 sprig rosemary
  • 200g split red lentils, well rinsed
  • 1 litre chicken stock
  • 4 tsp lemon juice
  • 100g cavolo nero, stalks removed
  • 90g ham hock, cubed or pulled

    • Method

    1. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat. Add the leek, celery, carrot and rosemary along with a pinch of salt. cover and gently sweat for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Uncover and fry for another minute, then stir in the rinsed lentils

    2. Add the stock and 250ml water, bring to the boil, then simmer gently for about 15-20 minutes, or until the lentils are soft. Remove the rosemary, then stir in 2 tsp lemon juice and check the seasoning

    3. Heat the remaining 1/2 tbsp oil in a frying pan over a high heat. Chop or tear the cavolo nero leaves into 3-4cm strips, then add to the pan with a little salt (not too much!) and the remaining 2tsp lemon juice. Fry, tossing regularly, for about 3-4 minutes, until just turning crisp. Serve the soup topped with the ham hock and crispy cavolo nero
