Chicken Vindaloo

Marinade Ingredients

  • 6 tbsp supermarket chilli powder, not proper stuff! (yes, 6 tablespoons!)
  • 2 tsp coriander seeds
  • 3 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4 cloves
  • 4 cardamom pods (green)
  • 2 tsp cinnamon powder
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 tbsp fresh ginger, roughly chopped
  • 10 garlic cloves (yes, 10!)
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 7 tbsp white vinegar
  • 250 ml water

    • Curry Sauce Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • Thumb sized piece of fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 4 tbsp tomato puree
  • 10 curry leaves
  • 250 ml chicken stock
  • Small pack of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 peppers, sliced
  • Chilli powder to taste
  • Salt to taste

    • Marinade Method

    1. Trim and cube the chicken and place into a large bowl. Place marinade ingredients in a small food processor with the water. Blitz until smooth and pour over chicken.

    2. Cover the bowl with cling film and then marinate for a minimum of 2 hours in the fridge. (Note: marinade is fairly thin - the water cooks down during slow cooking.)

    3. Clean the food processor bowl to use in the next step.

    Curry Sauce Method

    1. Preheat oven to 190 degrees C (170 degrees C fan).

    2. Add oil to a large ovenproof saucepan (e.g. a cast iron pan). Cook the onion, ginger and garlic until they become translucent.

    3. Add the mustard seeds then cook until onion is tinged with gold. Stir in tomato puree, cook for 1 minute.

    4. Add the chicken stock and stir. Transfer into the food processor to puree until smooth. Pour back into the pot.

    5. Add the chicken, marinade and curry leaves, stir then bring to simmer.

    6. Cover with a lid, then place in the oven for 50 minutes.

    7. Fry the peppers and mushrooms until soft.

    8. Return the chicken and sauce to the stove and reduce on a low heat. Add the peppers and mushrooms once the sauce is reduced.
