Chicken Dhansak

NOTE: Requires: Curry Base Sauce!

For the Chicken

  • 4 or 5 Chicken Breasts cut into chunks
  • Vegetable Oil
  • 1 tsp Turmeric
  • 4 tbsp Reserved Curry Sauce

    • For the Dhal

  • 8 heaped tbsp red split lentils
  • 900ml water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • 2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala

    • For the Curry

  • Vegetable oil or ghee for cooking
  • 500ml curry sauce
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 2 green chillies, finely chopped
  • 3 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • Lemon juice to taste
  • Salt to taste

    • Method

    1. Place the oil, turmeric and curry sauce into a large saucepan and mix well. Cook on a medium heat, stirring continuously until the sauce begins to darken (4 to 5 minutes).

    2. Add the chicken and stir well until all the pieces are well coated with the sauce. Cook until the chicken is just done. Remove the chicken from the pan and keep on a plate or in a dish.


    3. Add the lentils, water and salt into a saucepan and bring to the boil. Turn down the heat and simmer uncovered, skimming off any froth for the first 20 minutes. After this time partly cover the saucepan and cook for approximately 1 hour. After this time you should have a pale yellow, soup like consistency.

    4. While the dhal is cooking, fry the onion and garlic in the ghee until the onion is pale brown. Add the turmeric and garam masala to the onion and cook for 2 to 3 seconds. Stir the onion mixture into the cooked lentils.


    5. Heat the oil in a large saucepan, add the curry sauce and lentils. Bring to the boil. Add the chicken, chilli powder and green chilli, reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook until the sauce thickens, stirring regularly.

    6. Stir in the garam masala, ground cumin and lemon juice to taste. Salt to taste.

    7. Serve with basmati rice and poppadums.
